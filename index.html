Catnip Catcher

Catnip Catcher is a prototype game that I started working on solo the summer of my Sophomore year. It is made using the 2D template of Unity, using Unity's built in C# functions as well as a few of my own. Catnip Catcher is a 2D overhead perspective game where the player controls one of two cats. The goal is to get 15 bags of catnip as one these cats without losing all 9 lives. While trying to get the catnip, your owner is trying to catch you. As more bags are gotten, more people appear to try and catch you and their speed increases.

SideQuests

SideQuests was a group project during my second semester sophomore year for my Software Engineering class. This class taught skills of teamwork and development methods as well as working on a project as a team using the Agile Method. SideQuests was an MVP website that would "Gameify" completing tasks and goals that the user had set. Completion of tasks would reward points and rewards depending on the amount of points a user has. CarGame

CarGame is a work in progress group project over during my first semester of junior year for my Virtual Worlds class. This class goes over game development using the Unity game engine, with assignments throughout the semester showing of those skills learned. CarGame is a survival zombie driving game where the goal is to kill as many zombies within 5 minutes. The player controls a truck with a plow that kills the zombies on impact at a certain speed. during the course of the game there are power-ups spawned which either give more health to the player or explode the car and zombies around it. There are two types of zombies, normal zombies and bomb zombies, hitting the bomb zombies have the same effect as the bomb power-up.

I worked on the car movement, choppy at first but later smoothed out, and the title screen.

I am currently working on a new multiplayer feature using Unity's Netcode for GameObjects. I will also be working on turning the original single player game into an Entity Component System with Unity's Data Oriented Technology Stack rather than it being an Object Oriented. This is for an independent study under Professor Ashish Amresh.
